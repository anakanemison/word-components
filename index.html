English Word Parts Atlas

A searchable guide to English roots, prefixes, suffixes, and common combining forms. This version is intentionally broad: it covers classroom staples, productive everyday affixes, and many of the classical elements that show up in academic, scientific, and literary vocabulary.

Origins are simplified into broad families such as Latin, Greek, and Old English / Germanic. In real word history, many forms passed through French or scholarly Latin before settling into English.

How To Decode a New Word

1. Spot the pieces Look for a beginning chunk, a middle root, and an ending.
2. Start with the root The root usually carries the core idea, while prefixes and suffixes refine it.
3. Test the whole meaning Combine the parts and see whether the guess makes sense in context.
